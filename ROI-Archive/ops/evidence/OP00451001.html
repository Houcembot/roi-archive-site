REQUEST FOR EXPRESSIONS OF INTEREST (CONSULTING SERVICES – FIRMS SELECTION) Country: Republic of Uzbekistan Credit: IDA Credit No. 7787-UZ Project: Geospatial Infrastructure for Sustainable Territorial Development Assignment Title: Social Studies and User Surveys (Preliminary) Reference No.: GISTD/C1/CQS/02 1. Background The Government of the Republic of Uzbekistan has received financing from the International Development Association (IDA) under Financing Agreement No. 7787-UZ dated June 25, 2025, for the implementation of the Geospatial Infrastructure for Sustainable Territorial Development (GISTD) Project. The Project aims to improve the availability and accessibility of geospatial data in Uzbekistan through the establishment and operationalization of the National Spatial Data Infrastructure (UZ-NSDI), enhancement of geospatial data management systems, development of geoportals and geospatial services, strengthening of institutional capacities, and implementation of pilot activities in Samarkand city. As part of Component C – Institutional Capacity Enhancement and Project Management, the Project envisages conducting social studies and user surveys to support citizen engagement, assess inclusiveness and accessibility of geospatial services, establish baseline values for selected Project indicators, and provide analytical inputs for monitoring Project results. The Cadastre Agency under the National Committee for Sustainable Development of Urbanization and Housing Market of the Republic of Uzbekistan intends to apply part of the proceeds of the financing to engage a consulting firm to conduct a baseline (preliminary) social study and user survey under the Project. 2. Objective of the Assignment The objective of the assignment is to conduct a baseline social study and user survey under the Geospatial Infrastructure for Sustainable Territorial Development Project in order to establish baseline values against which Project progress, results, and impacts will be measured during implementation. The assignment shall provide baseline information on awareness, accessibility, use, satisfaction, inclusiveness, and citizen engagement related to geospatial and cadastral services, and shall generate baseline data for those indicators of the Project Results Framework that can be informed through survey-based data collection. The assignment shall be completed within a maximum period of eighty (80) working days from the date of contract signature. 3. Scope of Services The consulting services shall include, but not be limited to, the following: Part A – Survey Design and Preparation - Development of the survey methodology and sampling strategy; - Identification of target groups and reference (control) groups; - Development, testing, and finalization of survey instruments; - Preparation of fieldwork procedures, quality assurance arrangements, and implementation plans; - Conduct of pilot testing and revision of survey instruments based on pilot results. Part B – Data Collection and Analysis - Conduct of quantitative and qualitative data collection in Tashkent and Samarkand; - Supervision and quality control of fieldwork activities; - Data cleaning, validation, and processing; - Analysis of survey findings, including gender-disaggregated and stakeholder-specific analysis; - Assessment of awareness, accessibility, use, satisfaction, inclusiveness, citizen engagement, and barriers affecting the use of geospatial services; - Establishment of baseline values for relevant indicators of the Project Results Framework. Part C – Reporting and Knowledge Transfer - Preparation of draft and final analytical reports; - Preparation of datasets, codebooks, analytical tables, and supporting materials; - Presentation of findings to the Client and key stakeholders; - Incorporation of comments received from the Client and the World Bank into final deliverables. All deliverables shall be prepared in English and Uzbek and submitted in editable formats (Word, Excel/CSV) and PDF. Final acceptance of the assignment shall be subject to approval of the deliverables by the Client. 4.
